## Our Story

**harness.design** was founded by Avario, a developer and UI/UX designer, and Sam, a product designer.

The idea came from a real project. In April 2024, Sam was working on the wiring harness for an electric motorbike being designed for a New Zealand startup called Arlington. When it came time to design the harness, he went looking for the right tool, and found nothing that actually fit the job.

Most harness design software is built for large enterprise teams. The tools are expensive, complex, and often rely on table-driven workflows rather than visual interaction. Many feel like legacy desktop applications that haven't evolved in years. There was nothing that worked well for independent builders, hobbyists, or anyone designing a wiring harness outside of a large engineering team.

The gap was obvious. In many other industries, professional software has gradually become more accessible, CAD is the clearest example, where tools once reserved for large companies are now in the hands of individuals and small teams. That democratisation had never really happened in the wiring harness space.

The design philosophy was inspired by PCB design tools like KiCad. Just as PCB software separates the logical schematic from the physical board layout, harness.design is built around the same principle, keeping the electrical design and the physical harness distinct but connected, so you can move fluidly between the two.

Development started around mid-2025, and the beta launched at the end of December 2025. Since then, a growing community has formed around the tool, spanning a surprisingly wide range of builders and disciplines. Much of the development direction comes directly from that community, ensuring the tool keeps solving real problems for the people actually using it.

The long-term goal is to make a powerful harness design tool accessible to the people who actually need it, from garage hackers and grassroots motorsport builders, all the way up to professional race shops who want a modern alternative to legacy enterprise software.
