# Mates

A mate is when a [Connector](/components/component-connector) or [Terminal](/components/component-terminal) is plugged into each other. Mates are used to split harness into smaller sub-harness or to plug a harness into a [Device](/parts/part-device) represented by a [Group](/components/component-group). A bulkhead connector is a common example of a mate. Matted connectors are sometimes called pass-through connectors.

## Add a Mate
In the [Schematic View](/quick-start/schematic) mate a Connector or Terminal by dragging from the handle on the left or right side of the component label to the corresponding handle of another Connector or Terminal.
<br />

## Mates on the Layout View
Mates can be viewed and deleted on the [Layout View](/quick-start/layout) but cannot be added. Go to the [Schematic View](/quick-start/schematic) to add mates.

## Mating Connectors
When connectors are mated together the cavities are mapped one to one and are considered on the same electrical net. If the mate doesn't follow the following rules an error will be displayed:
