# Mates

A mate is used to show that two [Connectors](/component/connector) are plugged into eachother, or two or more [Terminals](/component/terminal) are connected.

A bulkhead connector is a common example of a mate. Mated connectors are sometimes called pass-through connectors.

## Adding a Mate on the Schematic View

To mate a [Connector](/component/connector) `left-click-drag` from the handle on the left or right side of the connector label to the corresponding handle of another Connector or Terminal.

## Mating Connectors

When connectors are mated together the cavities are mapped one to one and are considered on the same electrical net. If the mate doesn't meet the following rules, an error will be displayed:

<br />

  <thead>
    <Table.Tr>
      <Table.Th>Error</Table.Th>
      <Table.Th>Rule</Table.Th>
    </Table.Tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <Table.Tr>
      <Table.Td>Cavities do not match</Table.Td>
      <Table.Td>Mated connectors must have the same number of cavities</Table.Td>
    </Table.Tr>
    <Table.Tr>
      <Table.Td>Genders do not match</Table.Td>
      <Table.Td>Mated connectors with parts applied that have a gender must be opposing</Table.Td>

  </tbody>
</Table>
