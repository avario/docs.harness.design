# Mates

A mate is when a [Connector](/component/connector) or [Terminal](/component/terminal) is plugged into each other. Mates are used to split harness into smaller sub-harness or to plug a harness into a [Device](/part/device) represented by a [Group](/component/group). A bulkhead connector is a common example of a mate. Matted connectors are sometimes called pass-through connectors.

## Add a Mate on the Schematic view
To mate a [Connector](/component/connector) `left-click-drag` from the handle on the left or right side of the Conenctor label to the corresponding handle of another Connector or Terminal.
<br />

To mate a [Terminal](/component/terminal) hold down `shift` and `left-click-drag` from the handle on the left or right side of the terminal to another Connector or Terminal.

## Mating Connectors
When connectors are mated together the cavities are mapped one to one and are considered on the same electrical net. If the mate doesn't follow the following rules an error will be displayed:
