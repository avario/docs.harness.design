# <IconHistory/>Revisions<ProBadge/>

Revisions are named snapshots that mark milestones in your harness's history. Create one each time you finish a meaningful piece of work — a completed subsystem, a design review, a released version — to capture that point and label what it represents. You can always come back to a revision later to review it, or roll your working version back to it.

Open them from <IconMenu2/>**Main Menu** → <IconHistory/>**Revisions**.

<Callout>
  Revisions are available to anyone who can **edit** the harness. Viewers with read-only access
  don't see the Revisions menu. **Creating** a revision additionally requires a Pro or Enterprise
  plan — on the Free plan you can still view and restore existing revisions.
</Callout>

## Current working version

The top of the list is always your live, editable harness. A badge tells you whether it has changes that aren't captured in your most recent revision:

- **New changes** — you've edited the harness since the last revision was created.
- **No new changes** — your work matches the latest revision.

## Creating a revision

When you've finished a piece of work, click <IconPlus/>**Create revision** on the current working version, add an optional message describing what you've completed, and confirm. The snapshot captures the harness exactly as it is at that moment.

## Viewing a revision

Click <IconExternalLink/>**View revision** to open it in a new browser tab. The revision opens **read-only** — you can pan, inspect parts and export it, but not edit it — so opening one never affects your working version.

## Restoring a revision

Click <IconHistory/>**Restore revision** to roll your working version back to that snapshot. You'll be asked to confirm, because **your current work is overwritten**.

<Callout type="warning">
  Restoring replaces your current working version. To keep your current work, create a revision
  before restoring.
</Callout>

Each revision shows the date and time it was created and the name of the team member who created it.
