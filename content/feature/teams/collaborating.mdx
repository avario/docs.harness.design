# Collaborating on Harnesses

A harness that belongs to a team can be opened and edited by **every seated member** — not just its original owner.

## Moving a harness into your team

Moving a harness into your team **doesn't change who owns it**. You keep ownership and full control — you can still rename, share, restore, or remove it from the team whenever you like. Moving it simply lets the team's other seated members open and edit it alongside you.

There are two ways to move one of your personal harnesses into the team — you need to hold a seat for either:

- **From the open harness** — go to <IconMenu2/>**Main Menu** → <IconSettings/>**Harness settings** → <IconUsersGroup/>**Team** and choose **Move to team**.
- **From the Open dialog** — go to <IconMenu2/>**Main Menu** → <IconFolder/>**Open**, find the harness in the <IconUserCircle/>**Your harnesses** tab, open its <IconDotsVertical/>**options** menu and choose **Move to team**.

<br />
<img src="/feature/move-to-team.png" width={600} style={{ borderRadius: "8px" }} />

You can also create a team harness from scratch: in <IconMenu2/>**Main Menu** → <IconFolder/>**Open**, switch to the **Team** tab and use the **New team harness** tile.

<Callout>
  Team harnesses appear in the **Team** tab of the Open dialog for everyone on the team, alongside
  the name of the member who owns each one.
</Callout>

## Editing together

Everyone with a seat can have a team harness open at the same time — there's no checking out or locking to work around. You always know what your teammates are up to:

- When someone else has the harness open, an indicator shows who's also working on it.
- A **syncing indicator** appears whenever a teammate is making changes, and your view updates automatically when they save — no need to refresh.

<br />
<img src="/feature/presence-indicator.png" width={600} style={{ borderRadius: "8px" }} />

Because of this, editing together stays smooth even when you're both in the same harness at once — the indicators make it easy to see what's happening and stay out of each other's way.

<Callout>
  If two people edit the same harness at the very same moment, the most recent save is the one
  that's kept — so it's worth a quick glance at the indicators before reworking something a teammate
  is actively editing.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
  To safeguard a finished piece of work from later changes — and to keep a record of how the harness
  evolved — create a [revision](/feature/revisions). A revision is a named snapshot you can always
  restore, independent of anyone's later edits, making it the best way to mark milestones on a shared
  harness.
</Callout>

## Removing a harness from the team

From the same <IconUsersGroup/>**Team** settings, choose **Remove from team** to return the harness to personal ownership. A document's owner can always do this; the team owner can remove any of the team's harnesses.
